Create an Authority Document so that you can link Citations to it. Authority Documents can be a combination of statuses, regulations, directives, principles, standards, best practices, policies, and procedures.

<hint type="info">
  To create or edit an Authority Document, you must log in to \{\{Primary.Product}} as a **GRC Manager**.
</hint>

**To create an Authority Document:**

1. Open the **Authority Document** workspace.
2. Click **New GRC Authority Document** to open a blank **Authority Document** form.
3. Enter the information into the fields as required.
   - **Owner Email** and **Sponsor Email** auto-populate based on the **Owner** and **Sponsor** fields.
4. In the **Details** tab, enter a **Document Title**. You can also add attachments in the **Details** tab.
5. Click **Save**.
6. Use the **Supervisory Authorities**, **Citations**, and **Controls** tabs to link existing records, or to create new associated records for the Authority Document.

<Paragraph indent="1">
  Use the **Journals** tab to add new Journal notes for the Authority Document.
</Paragraph>

7. Click **Save**.

<hint type="info" indent="1">
  Click **Refresh** If changes you made or relevant tabs are not shown in the record after you have saved it.
</hint>

## Edit an Authority Document

**To edit an Authority Document:**

1. Double-click the Authority Document to open the details.
2. Edit the information as required.
3. Click **Save**.
