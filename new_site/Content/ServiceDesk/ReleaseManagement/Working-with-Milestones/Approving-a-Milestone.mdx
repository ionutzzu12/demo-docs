When the status of a major release is updated to active, the approval workflow starts. After the first milestone in the sequence is approved and its status is updated to complete, the next milestone in the sequence will start. After all the milestones defined for a release are approved, the release status is updated to closed.

Depending on how the workflow is configured for the different major releases (by the different combinations of scope and phase), a milestone can be approved by a user or a group. When a group is configured to approve the milestone, the milestone is approved after all the approvers have approved it (default), or 51% of them have approved it (if configured).

By default, emergency and minor release types are not part of the approval process.

If you are set up as an approver for a release milestone, you are notified when the status for the default task of the article belonging to that specific collection is updated to pending approval. ## Approval through Email

You can accept or reject a milestone by responding to it directly from the email that \{\{FRS\_VARS.ProductName}} sends you. When you receive a milestone approval email request, do the following:

1. Select **Reply** and indicate your acceptance or rejection of the article by entering **approved** or **denied** in the body of the message. Do not modify the subject line of the email when sending your response through email.
2. If you reject an article, enter the reason for rejecting it in the second line of the body of the message.

<hint type="info" indent="1">
  The approval keywords are configured in the **FRS Approval Status Keyword** tab records in the email configuration form. By default, the keywords to approve or reject via email are approved or denied.
</hint>

3. Select **Send**.

The milestone record status is not updated until all of the approvers identified for the specific milestone have responded, unless the approval workflow is configured so that you are the only approver. By default, a release milestone is not approved until all the approvers approve it.

The **Approval Vote Tracking** record for the approval instance of the milestone record is updated to show your response.

## Approval using the Self-Service Portal

You can either select the link that appears in the email to go to the Self-Service Portal or log directly into the Self-Service Portal.

<hint type="info">
  If you use the Self-Service Portal for approvals, you must be in the Self Service role. Contact your administrator to add the Self Service role to your employee profile if you are not able to log into the Self-Service Portal.
</hint>

1. Sign into the **Self-Service Portal**.
2. Select **My Items**.
3. Select **Approval Request** from the drop-down list.
4. Select **Approval Request for Release**.
5. Either approve or deny the request:
   - If you approve the request, select **Approve**, and then select **Approve** again to dismiss the approval confirmation message and confirm the approval.
   - If you deny the request, select **Deny**, and then enter the reason for denying the request in the **Deny Approval-Reason** field and select **Submit**.
6. Select **Approve**.

The milestone record status is not updated until all the approvers identified for the specific milestone have responded, unless the approval workflow is configured so that you are the only approver. By default, a release milestone is not approved until all the approvers approve it.

The system updates the **Approval Vote Tracking** record for the approval instance of the milestone record to show your response.
