\{\{FRS\_VARS.Company-short}} \{\{FRS\_VARS.Neurons\_for\_PPM}} allows \{\{FRS\_VARS.Neurons\_ITSM\_-\_Project\_Manager}}s and \{\{FRS\_VARS.Neurons\_ITSM\_-\_Portfolio\_Manager}}s to create Skills and assign them to Resources or Project Roles. Refer to [Working with Project Roles](<Working with Project Roles.mdx>). Project and \{\{FRS\_VARS.Neurons\_ITSM\_-\_Portfolio\_Manager}}s can search for Resources with the Skills required by Project Tasks.

## Creating a Skill

To create a Skill:

1. Click **More...** in the main menu bar.
2. Click **Project Skills** to open the **Project Skills** workspace.
3. Click **New Project Skill** to open the **New Project Skill** form.
4. Enter the name of the new **Skill**, and click **Save**.

## Linking Skills

After creating Skills, link them to Resources and Roles. This allows you to match the Skills required for a Project with the appropriate Resources.

### Linking Skills to a Resource

After creating Skills, link them to individual resources.

1. Double-click on a skill from the Project Skills workspace list view.
2. Click **Link** on the **Employees** tab to search for an employee.
3. Select **Custom Search**, and enter your search parameters.
4. Click **Search**.
5. Click the name of the employee, and then click **Select** to link the skill to the employee.

### Linking Skills to a Project Role

Often a Skill applies to everyone assigned a certain Project Role. For example, you might apply the skill "SQL Server" to all resources in the "Database Administrator" role. When you assign a Role to a Resource, the Skills applied to that Role automatically transfer to the Resource.

<hint type="info">
  When you remove a Role from a Resource, the Skills applied through that Role remain applied to the Resource.
</hint>

1. Double-click on a Skill from the **Project Skills** workspace list view.
2. Click **Link** on the **Project Roles** tab.
3. Click **Project Role**, then click **Select**.
