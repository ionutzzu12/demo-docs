Everything within \{\{FRS\_VARS.ProductName}} is driven by business objects.

## Creating Business Objects

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Creating a Business Object

  1. From the Configuration console, select **Build > Business Objects**. The application displays the **Business Objects** workspace.
  2. From the right panel in the **Create an Object** pane, select **New Object**. The application displays the **Business Object: Create New** page.
  3. Choose an option:
     - **Stand-alone Business Object**: A simple business object where you can define business object fields. Once created, you can extend it. Refer to :Link[Creating a Stand-Alone Business Object]{label="Creating a Stand-Alone Business Object" overridedLabel="Creating a Stand-Alone Business Object" spaceId docId="z5Hl_JIl1C8b9P5aZK1aD" version="v2" docAnchorId="#Create_a_Stand-alone_Business_Object" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="Using Business Objects.mdx"}.
     - **Group Business Object**: A business object that is the base for a group of related business objects. It defines the properties which are common to the business objects in the group. Refer to :Link[Creating a Group Business Object]{label="Creating a Group Business Object" overridedLabel="Creating a Group Business Object" spaceId docId="z5Hl_JIl1C8b9P5aZK1aD" version="v2" docAnchorId="#Creating_a_Group_Business_Object" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="Using Business Objects.mdx"}.
     - **Extension To Group Business Object**: A business object that extends the properties and behavior of another business object. You can add specific properties and business rules. Refer to :Link[Creating an Extension to Existing Business Objects]{label="Creating an Extension to Existing Business Objects" overridedLabel="Creating an Extension to Existing Business Objects" spaceId docId="z5Hl_JIl1C8b9P5aZK1aD" version="v2" docAnchorId="#Create_an_Extension_to_an_Existing_Business_Object" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="Using Business Objects.mdx"}.
     - **Materialized View Business Object**: A complex type of business object. Once created, you can set up to update data from different sources. Refer to :Link[Creating a Materialized View Business Object]{label="Creating a Materialized View Business Object" overridedLabel="Creating a Materialized View Business Object" spaceId docId="z5Hl_JIl1C8b9P5aZK1aD" version="v2" docAnchorId="#Materialized_View_Business_Object" loadingMethod="dynamic" newTab="false" href="Using Business Objects.mdx"}.

  ## Creating a Group Business Object

  A group business object consists of an abstract base type and one or more derived types. A configuration item is one example of a group business object type. The configuration item group subtypes, such as workstations, servers, printers, and so on, derive fields and relationships from the configuration item base type. This allows you, for example, to use a search to gather information about all the derived business objects and display them in a list or on a dashboard. For example, printer, workstation, server, and so on are all separate business objects. Without connecting these different business objects under a group, you cannot search across, or list these business objects together. Connecting them to the group business object as derived business objects allows you to do this.

  In past releases it was sometimes possible to use abstract base type in business logic, without defining any derived types. Beginning with \{\{FRS\_VARS.ProductName}} Release 2016.1, this is no longer allowed.

## Creating an Extension to Existing Business Objects

<ExpandableHeading>
  ## Creating an Extension to a Single Business Object

  You can embed a single business object into a parent business object. For example, the group business object called **Task** contains a set of common fields (business properties), such as:

  - EstimatedEffort
  - ActualStartDate
  - EmailAddress

  However, you can create one or more extensions of **Task**, extended business objects that are member business objects. The base business object (**Task**) has its business properties extended into these business objects without recreating the fields separately for each extended business object. The extended business objects can then gather more specific information, such as:

  - **Task.Assignment**: Contains fields such as PlannedCost, AssignmentID, and AssignerEmail.
  - **Task.WorkOrder**: Contains fields such as ActualCost, EstimatedCost, and WorkOrderID.

  An extended business object (such as **Task.WorkOrder**) can be a member of a business object group.
</ExpandableHeading>

  To add a custom tab to a business object

  1. From the Configuration console, select **Build > Business Objects**. The application displays the **Business Objects** workspace.

  8. In the **Object** column, select a plugin from the drop-down list.
  9. Select **Save**.
  10. Return to the \{\{FRS\_VARS.ProductName}} Application and refresh the browser.
  11. Verify that the new custom tab displays.
